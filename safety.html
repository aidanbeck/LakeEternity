Safety On The Lake

Rules & Regulations

We want you to have fun, LEGAL fun! Our rules and regulations are very important here at Lake Eternity, so to maximize FUN we suggest you pay close attention!

Rules:

  • You must have a drivers license and be at least 16 years of age to operate vehicles on the water with a length greater than 1 meter.
  • Always give right of way to boats flying a crimson red flag & white cross design. Do not discuss sightings of these boats with strangers. Do not approach them.
  • Keep noise at a reasonable level between the hours of 11PM and 4AM, 11AM and 1PM, as well as 6PM and 6:15PM.
  • NO fishing absolutely NONE do NOT fish EVER.
  • Do not operate a vehicle larger than 1 meter while intoxicated.
  • Life jackets are prohibited after 9PM.

* Laws are enforced by the Clark Lifeguarding Division.

Safety Tips!

Here are some helpful guidelines to keep yourself and your family safe on the lake!

  • Wear sunscreen diligently! Yes, even when it's cloudy!
  • Always supervise children aged 0-10 and 12-15. 11 year olds can fend for themselves.
  • Avoid life jackets! In emergencies, they often get in the way of your ability to swim. Big Jacket is lying to you!
  • If you see anything abnormal, contact the Clark Lifeguarding Division!
  • If you have an emergency, contact the Clark Lifeguarding Division!
  • To report a missing person, contact the Clark Lifeguarding Division! It is imperative that you do not contact any other organization as they will not be as efficient as the Clark Lifeguarding Division and are likely to just get in the way.
  • Wait at least 30 minutes after eating before swimming!
